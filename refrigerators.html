Maintenance tips for prolonging the life of your refrigerator

Hey there! Are you looking for some maintenance tips to keep your refrigerator running smoothly and lasting longer? Well, look no further! Here's a few things you can do to help prolong the life of your fridge.

First off, make sure to clean the coils on the back of your refrigerator regularly. This will help it run more efficiently and prevent any build-up of dust or dirt that can cause it to overheat.

Another tip is to check the door seals for any signs of wear or damage. If they're not sealing properly, cold air can escape and make your fridge work harder to stay cold. You can easily test this by placing a dollar bill in the seal and closing the door - if you can pull it out easily, it's time to replace the seal.

Also, try not to overload your refrigerator with too much food. This can strain the compressor and cause it to wear out faster. Make sure there's enough space between items for air to circulate properly.

Lastly, don't forget to defrost your freezer regularly if it's not frost-free. A build-up of ice can decrease efficiency and make your fridge work harder than necessary.

So there you have it - a few simple tips to help keep your refrigerator in tip-top shape for years to come. Remember, a little maintenance goes a long way in keeping your appliances running smoothly!

How often should I defrost my freezer in my refrigerator?
Manual-defrost freezers should be defrosted whenever ice buildup reaches about 1/4 inch thickness, typically every 6 months to maintain optimal performance. Some newer models have automatic defrost features that eliminate the need for manual defrosting.
